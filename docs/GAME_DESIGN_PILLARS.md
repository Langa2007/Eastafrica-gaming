# Game Design Pillars

## Working Title

East Africa Truck Simulator

This can change later, but the working title keeps the project focused on its strongest market gap.

## Core Promise

Build a trucking simulator where African and Asian trucking routes are not side content. They are the main event.

The first serious region should be East Africa:

- Nairobi
- Kampala
- Kigali
- Dar es Salaam
- Mombasa
- Arusha
- Kisumu
- Eldoret
- Juba as a later expansion candidate

## Player Fantasy

The player is not just driving a truck. They are building a transport business across difficult, beautiful, unpredictable roads.

They should feel:

- The satisfaction of long-haul driving.
- The pressure of damaged roads and fragile cargo.
- The pride of upgrading a truck from basic workhorse to trusted machine.
- The business tension of fuel, maintenance, contracts, border fees, driver wages, and route risk.
- The cultural specificity of East African roads, cities, landscapes, radio, signage, depots, and roadside life.

## Four Design Pillars

### 1. Underserved Geography

The game must own the routes other trucking sims have ignored.

Priority order:

1. East Africa core corridor: Nairobi, Kampala, Kigali, Dar es Salaam, Mombasa.
2. West Africa expansion: Lagos, Accra, Abidjan, Cotonou.
3. Southeast Asia expansion: Bangkok, Ho Chi Minh City, Kuala Lumpur, Phnom Penh.

### 2. Meaningful Roads

Roads should change gameplay, not just scenery.

Route types:

- Relaxing highways.
- Dense city deliveries.
- Rough rural roads.
- Dirt tracks.
- Mountain climbs and descents.
- Border-crossing routes.
- Seasonal hazard routes.

### 3. Trucking As A Business

The player should grow from solo driver to fleet operator.

Business systems:

- Contracts.
- Fuel costs.
- Maintenance.
- Repairs.
- Driver hiring.
- AI driver assignments.
- Regional supply and demand.
- Garage expansion.
- Fleet specialization.

### 4. Community And Modding

The community should be able to extend the game.

Long-term requirements:

- Mod-ready vehicle definitions.
- Mod-ready cargo definitions.
- Route and depot data files.
- Skin support.
- Basic map-editing tools.
- Steam Workshop support when the project reaches Steam readiness.

## Tone And Authenticity

The game should feel respectful, grounded, and specific.

Avoid turning African roads into stereotypes. Difficult roads, border friction, informal negotiations, and bad weather can be part of the game, but they must be balanced by beauty, competence, humor, music, roadside commerce, and the pride of regional trucking culture.

## First Playable Loop

The first playable version should include:

1. Choose a delivery job.
2. Spawn at a depot.
3. Drive to destination.
4. Manage fuel and fatigue.
5. Keep cargo condition acceptable.
6. Deliver cargo.
7. Earn money and XP.
8. Return to the job board.

No advanced AI, no multiplayer, no real map scale, and no high-end graphics are needed for the first loop.
